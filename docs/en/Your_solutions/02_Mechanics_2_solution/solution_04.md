### **Problem Setup**

* **Mass 1 ($m_1$):** 0.5 kg
* **Initial height ($h$):** 3.0 m
* **Mass 2 ($m_2$):** 1.5 kg (initially at rest)
* **Gravity ($g$):** 9.8 m/s²

---

### **Step 1: Conservation of Energy (Block 1)**

As the 0.5 kg block slides down the frictionless track, its initial gravitational potential energy is converted entirely into kinetic energy at the bottom. 

$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

We can cancel $m_1$ from both sides and solve for the block's velocity ($v_1$) just before the collision:

$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v_1^2$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Plug in the given values:

$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8} \approx 7.668 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### **Step 2: Conservation of Momentum (The Collision)**

When the 0.5 kg block strikes the 1.5 kg block and they stick together, it is a perfectly inelastic collision. Because the track is frictionless, the system is isolated, and total momentum is conserved.

$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_{2i} = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Since block 2 is initially at rest, its initial velocity ($v_{2i}$) is 0, which eliminates the second term on the left side:

$$m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Now, solve for the final combined velocity ($v_f$):

$$v_f = \frac{m_1v_1}{m_1 + m_2}$$

Substitute the calculated and known values:

$$v_f = \frac{0.5 \cdot 7.668}{0.5 + 1.5}$$
$$v_f = \frac{3.834}{2.0}$$
$$v_f = 1.917 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### **Final Answer**

The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is **1.92 m/s**.
