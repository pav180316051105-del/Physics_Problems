# Problem 8: Lorentz Force

## Given:
* **Particle charge (q):** 2 × 10^-19 C (Coulombs)
* **Particle mass (m):** 4 × 10^-27 kg (This is extra, unnecessary information for this specific formula!)
* **Magnetic field (B):** 0.5 T (Teslas)
* **Velocity (v):** 10^6 m/s
* **Angle (α):** 90° (Because the problem states the particle enters "perpendicular" to the field)

**Find:** Lorentz Force (F) — ?

---

## Explanation of Concepts (in simple terms):

Before we calculate, let's understand what is actually happening.

1. **Magnetic Field (B):** Imagine this as an invisible flow, like a river's current. It is measured in Teslas (T). The higher the number, the "stronger" the field.
2. **Charge (q):** This is the property of the particle that makes it "feel" the magnetic field. If a particle has no charge (like a neutron), the magnetic field will simply ignore it.
3. **Lorentz Force (F):** This is the actual physical push or pull that the magnetic field exerts on the charged particle. **Important rule:** a magnetic field only pushes a particle *if it is moving*. It does nothing to a stationary charge.
4. **Why "perpendicular"?** This means the particle's path forms a 90-degree angle with the magnetic field lines. This angle determines how hard the field pushes. At exactly 90 degrees, the push is at its absolute maximum.
5. **Why is the mass (m) given?** It's a trap for beginners! We would only need the mass if the problem asked us to find the particle's *acceleration* or the *radius* of the circle it spins in. To find just the force itself, mass doesn't matter.

---

## Solution

To find the Lorentz force acting on a moving charged particle, we use this main formula:

**F = |q| · v · B · sin(α)**

### Step 1: Handle the sine function
Since the particle enters perpendicular to the field, the angle α is 90 degrees. 
In trigonometry, the sine of 90 degrees is exactly 1 (sin(90°) = 1). 
Because multiplying by 1 changes nothing, our formula simplifies to:

**F = |q| · v · B**

### Step 2: Substitute the values
Let's plug our numbers from the "Given" section into the formula:

F = (2 × 10^-19) · (10^6) · 0.5

### Step 3: Step-by-step Calculation
To avoid getting confused by the big numbers and negative exponents, let's group them: we will multiply the regular numbers together first, and the "powers of 10" together second.

1. **Multiply the regular numbers:**
   2 · 0.5 = 1

2. **Multiply the powers of 10:** The rule in math is that when you multiply numbers with the same base (10), you simply add their exponents together.
   (-19) + 6 = -13
   This gives us: 10^-13

3. **Put it all together:**
   F = 1 · 10^-13 N

---

## Conclusion
The magnetic field will push this charged particle with a force of **1 × 10^-13 Newtons**. While this seems like a tiny number to us, for a microscopic particle, it is a massive force that will instantly curve its path of motion!
